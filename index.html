About

I graduated from the University of Chicago (BS ‘21) with a degree in computer science and a minor in creative writing. I love both these things (computer science/programming, reading/writing/literature) and I do believe that, in some ways, they are not so different.

As a software engineer, I want to make things that are fast, robust, and fun to use. I'm particularly fascinated by the guts of software—the parts of code that run behind the scenes and make things tick. I'm also passionate about software for social and environmental good.

Within computer science, I'm primarily interested in distributed systems. As they relate to distributed systems, I'm also broadly interested in: databases, privacy/security, formal methods (e.g. for correctness and privacy), networks, and parallel computing. I hope to do work that makes distributed systems more usable, more secure, and more positively impactful.
