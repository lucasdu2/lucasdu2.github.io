About

I am an MS student at UC Davis studying computer science. Previously, I was a Technical Consultant at Hitachi Vantara, working on cloud storage at eBay. Even before that, I graduated from the University of Chicago (BS ‘21) with a degree in computer science and a minor in English and creative writing.

I’m interested in programming languages and formal methods research to both improve the quality of software systems and make it easier to develop higher quality software. More broadly, I’m interested in moving software development away from heuristics and hacks and onto solid, principled ground.

Research Interests

I’m primarily interested in applying techniques from programming languages and formal methods to software systems. My interests within this umbrella are fairly varied, but I’m currently excited about verification of concurrent/distributed systems and better programming models for the cloud, particularly related to distributed storage and stream processing.

I’m also generally interested in compilers, type systems, web browsers, scientific and high performance computing, security and cryptography, peer-to-peer and decentralized systems, data privacy, program synthesis, and so on. But life is finite. I do hope that anything I do with computers centers on some subset of these interests.

At a much higher and more idealistic level, I’m interested in software that is more empowering and less exploitative, extractive, or otherwise harmful. I want software that adds more to our lives and our world than it takes away.

Personal

I like reading short stories, writing fiction, and listening to music. I also like long form journalism and literary criticism. At one point I wanted to be a journalist. At another point, I wanted to be a writer. I like a lot of humanities-related things and it’s unfortunate that market forces have deemed most of them to be strictly less valuable than producing software.
