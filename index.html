About Me

I graduated from the University of Chicago (BS ‘21) with a degree in computer science and a minor in creative writing. I love both these things (computer science/programming, reading/writing/literature) and I believe that, in many ways, they are not so different.

As a software engineer, I want to make things that are fast, robust, and fun to use. I'm particularly fascinated by the guts of software—the parts of code that run behind the scenes and make things tick. I'm also passionate about software for social and environmental good.

Within computer science, I'm broadly interested in distributed systems. I hope to do work that makes distributed systems more reliable, more secure, more empowering to the user, and generally more positively impactful.

More specifically, I'm very interested in abstractions and techniques that make it easier to write correct and efficient distributed software. I believe that our tools could be helping us much more in writing error-free, understandable, and high performance programs.

I also like reading short stories, writing fiction, and listening to music, although those interests, unfortunately, are not as profitable as software.
