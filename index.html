About Me

I graduated from the University of Chicago (BS ‘21) with a degree in computer science and a minor in creative writing. I love both these things (computer science/programming, reading/writing/literature) and I believe that, in many ways, they are not so different.

As a software engineer, I want to make things that are fast, robust, and fun to use. I'm particularly fascinated by the guts of software—the parts of code that run behind the scenes and make things tick. I'm also passionate about non-harmful software for social and environmental good. Software should strive to be empowering, not to quietly exploit people and perpetuate inequity for profit.

Within computer science, I'm broadly interested in more reliable, more performant, more secure, and more private distributed systems. I'm specifically interested in more effective abstractions and techniques to make it much easier to write correct and efficient software, particularly concurrent, distributed software. I believe that our tools should be helping us much more in writing error-free, understandable, and high performance programs, and also that there is an increasingly dire need for such tools as software embeds itself more deeply into the fabric of our lives.

I also like reading short stories, writing fiction, and listening to music, among other things. Unfortunately, market forces have deemed most of those interests to be strictly less valuable than producing software.
