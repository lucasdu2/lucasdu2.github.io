About

I am an incoming computer science MS student at UC Davis. I’m currently taking a brief break between working full-time and starting school.

Previously, I worked on cloud storage at eBay. I have a BS in computer science from UChicago.

I’m interested in programming languages and formal methods research to improve the quality of software systems and make it easier to develop higher quality software. Software should be more empowering and less frustrating for both developers and users.

You can reach me at lzdu (at) ucdavis! I am also on Github and LinkedIn.

Research Interests

I’m primarily interested in applying techniques from programming languages and formal methods to software systems. I’m currently excited about:

  • verification of concurrent/distributed systems and
  • better programming models for the cloud, particularly related to distributed storage and stream processing.

More broadly, I want to help move software away from heuristics and hacks and onto more solid, elegant, and principled ground. I generally like any sort of work that explores the interplay between mathematics and computation, preferably with an eye to practical impact on real-world systems. I believe there is a lot of room for mathematical abstractions to give us much better results across the computing stack, particularly when it comes to correctness, security, privacy, flexibility, and performance. And as increasingly complex software starts to underpin more of our lives, we desperately need better results.
