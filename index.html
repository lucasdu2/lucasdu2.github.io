About

I graduated from the University of Chicago (BS ‘21) with a degree in computer science and a minor in creative writing. I love making things that are fast, robust, and fun to use. I'm particularly fascinated by the guts of software—the parts of code that run behind the scenes and make things tick. I'm also passionate about software for social and environmental good.

Within computer science, I'm primarily interested in distributed systems, networks, computer privacy/security (especially within large distributed systems), and parallel computing. I'm also interested in the mathematical provability of algorithms, such as provable correctness in distributed systems and provable privacy. I hope to do work that makes distributed systems more usable, more secure, and more positively impactful.

My other interests include listening to music, reading, and, at times, writing. My favorite song of all time is Step by Vampire Weekend. My second favorite song of all time is Runaway by Kanye West. One story I've read that has probably stuck with me the most (in the past 3 to 4 years) is Triumph Over the Grave by Denis Johnson.
