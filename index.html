About Me

I'm employed as a Technical Consultant at Hitachi Vantara. I work on cloud storage at eBay.

I graduated from the University of Chicago (BS ‘21) with a degree in computer science and a minor in creative writing. I love both these things (computer science/programming, reading/writing/literature) and I believe that, in many ways, they are not so different.

Interests

As a software engineer, I'm fascinated by the guts of software—the parts of code that run behind the scenes and make things tick. I'm also passionate about non-harmful software for social and environmental good. Software should strive to be empowering, not to quietly exploit people and perpetuate inequity for profit.

Within computer science, I'm broadly interested in more reliable, more performant, more secure, and more private distributed systems. It should generally be much easier to create high-quality, user-empowering concurrent and connected software. I believe we can accomplish this goal through more effective abstractions and techniques at a systems level, in our programming languages, operating systems, and databases.

Currently, some specific interests are:

  • Applications of formal methods to practical software, particularly distributed software
  • Better programming models for the cloud, especially related to distributed storage
  • Type systems for concurrent, distributed software
  • Software synthesis and verification
  • Techniques for private data analysis
  • Verification of trustworthy AI/ML system

I also like reading short stories, writing fiction, and listening to music, among other things. Unfortunately, market forces have deemed most of those interests to be strictly less valuable than producing software.
